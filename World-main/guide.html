🌍 World — Game Guide

A passive emergent-civilisation simulator. Watch primitive agents discover fire, language, and society — or fade from the earth.

Overview

World is a god-game / emergent-civilisation simulator. You are a silent observer watching small societies grow from nothing. Agents wander a procedurally generated landscape, satisfy basic needs, and independently discover a tree of civilisation concepts. Your role is to watch, click for details, and occasionally nudge through the settings. There is no direct control over individual agents.

The game ends when the last agent dies. Success is measured by how far your civilisation advanced — which concepts were discovered, how large the population grew, and how long it survived.

Running the Game

  1. Open a terminal in d:\Coding\World\
  2. Run:
    python -m http.server 8080
  3. Open http://localhost:8080 in a modern browser (Chrome or Edge recommended)
The game requires a local server because it fetches data/concepts.json. Opening index.html directly as a file will fail with a CORS error.

Speed Controls

Bottom-right buttons: ⏸ 1× 5× 20×

  • ⏸ Pause — freeze the simulation (camera still moves)
  • 1× — real-time (default, best for observation)
  • 5× — fast-forward to early discoveries
  • 20× — skip to mid-game concepts quickly; resource depletion and crashes are more likely
At 20× speed, many events fire simultaneously. The game is designed to handle this gracefully, but very large populations at extreme speed may show visual jitter.

Interacting with the World

Click an Agent

Click near any coloured figure to open the Agent Info Panel:

  • Name — procedurally generated (e.g. "Elorin")
  • State — current behaviour (see Agent States below)
  • Hunger / Energy bars — red = critical, orange = low
  • Age — current and maximum lifespan. Concepts extend max age.
  • Curiosity — discovery probability multiplier (30–80%)
  • Task — role assigned after Organisation is discovered
  • Knowledge tags — all concepts this agent knows

Click a Tile

Click an empty tile to open the Tile Info Panel:

  • Tile type, coordinates, and description
  • Food bar (Grass and Forest tiles only) — shows current resource level

Settings (☰)

  • Starting population — 5 to 100 agents; applies on the next reset
  • Max population — cap on carrying capacity (40–300); births blocked above this. Default 100.
  • ↺ Reset World — generates a new world with a new random seed; all progress is lost

The World

The world is a 32×32 tile grid generated procedurally from a random seed. Each run is different.

🌊
Water
Fish swim here. Impassable until Sailing is discovered. Shoreline tiles are discovery hotspots for Fishing and Sailing.
🌿
Grass
Primary food source. Berry bushes (~55% of tiles). Sheep and pigs graze here. Good spawn zone. Regenerates food over time.
🌲
Forest
Rich in food and discovery. Pine trees (~82% of tiles). Key site for Fire, Hunting, Clothing, Weaving, Rope, Medicine. Regenerates more slowly than Grass.
🪨
Stone
Rocky terrain. No food. Discovery site for Stone Tools, Metal Tools, Pottery. Agents pass through but don't gather here.
⛰️
Mountain
Impassable until Mountain Climbing is discovered. Snow-capped peaks. Discovery requires being adjacent (on Stone tiles) without stone_tools needed.

Agent System

Each agent is an autonomous entity with needs, knowledge, and a simple task they optimise for each tick.

Needs

  • Hunger (0–1): drains at 1/90 per game-second. Agents gather food when hunger drops below ~0.25 (lower if a Gatherer task).
  • Energy (0–1): drains at 1/200 per game-second, multiplied by the weather energy mult. Agents sleep when energy drops below ~0.2.

Agent States

StateColourMeaning
WanderingGrey-blueMoving to a random nearby tile; re-evaluates needs every 3–7 seconds. In cold/stormy weather without fire, biases toward Forest.
GatheringAmberHeading to the nearest food tile (Grass or Forest) within 8 tiles.
SleepingBlue-violetResting in place; energy recovers faster. Duration 10–18 game-seconds.
SocializingPurpleCurrently passing knowledge to or receiving it from a nearby agent. Happens automatically during all states.
DiscoveringOrange glowJust made a discovery. Brief pause then returns to Wandering. An orange flash appears at their location.

Life & Death

  • Agents are born with a max age of 60–120 game-seconds, extended by fire (×15%), shelter (×10%), cooking (×20%), and medicine (×25%).
  • With all four, a single agent can live up to 180 game-seconds — 3× the base lifespan.
  • Agents die of old age (health → 0). There is currently no starvation or disease death, though near-zero hunger causes very slow energy loss.

Reproduction

Two adults within 3.5 tiles who both have:

  • Hunger > 0.40 and Energy > 0.20
  • No active reproduction cooldown (18–38 game-seconds after last birth; shorter with Community)
  • Age ≥ 20 game-seconds (maturity)

…will produce a child at a point between them. The child starts as a juvenile (smaller sprite) and becomes adult at age 20.

Carrying Capacity

Maximum allowed living agents = min(max_pop_setting, max(40, 25 + food_tile_count × 0.18)), multiplied by 1.25 if Agriculture is known. The max cap is configurable in Settings (default 100, range 40–300). Births are blocked above this cap.

Tasks (requires Organisation)

Once any agent discovers Organisation, they adopt a specialisation role:

Gatherer
Gathers food proactively at hunger <0.55. +5% gather bonus.
📢
Teacher
Moves toward other agents to share knowledge. +10% spread rate.
🔭
Scout
Wanders up to 3 tiles further than normal. +15% discovery chance.
💚
Carer
Rests earlier (energy threshold 0.35) with +10% recovery speed.

The Concept Graph

Knowledge is represented as a directed graph of 26 concepts across two eras. Agents discover concepts when they satisfy conditions and pass a probability roll. Once known, concepts spread between agents who spend time near each other.

How Discovery Works

  1. Each tick, an agent checks every undiscovered concept.
  2. If prerequisites are met and all conditions pass, a roll of discovery_probability × curiosity × delta determines success.
  3. Scouts add +15%, Curiosity Culture adds +20%.
  4. If the agent is near a natural fire (lightning strike), fire discovery chance is ×30.

How Spreading Works

Every 4–6 game-seconds, agents within 5 tiles of each other socialise. For each concept one knows and the other doesn't, a roll of spread_rate × 4 per check determines transfer. Language doubles spread rate; Writing adds ×1.5 on top; Teacher task adds ×1.1.

Agents without fire, shelter, clothing, or housing lose energy faster in cold weather. Each protection concept reduces the multiplier:

  • 🔥 Fire: −0.25 from energy drain mult
  • 🏚️ Shelter: −0.35
  • 🧥 Clothing: −0.20
  • 🏠 Housing: −0.20
  • Floor: mult never drops below 1.0 (protections don't make things easier than clear weather)

Known Limitations & Future Plans

  • No starvation death — agents with zero hunger lose energy faster but don't die from hunger directly.
  • Wildlife not huntable — Hunting concept gives a gather bonus from Forest tiles but doesn't remove sheep/pigs from the world yet.
  • No buildings — concepts like Housing and Shelter don't yet place visible structures on the map.
  • No disease / disasters — EventSystem is planned but not yet implemented.
  • No save/load — SaveSystem is planned. Currently each session starts fresh.
  • Concept tree ends at Era 2 — Philosophy, governance, and further eras are planned.
